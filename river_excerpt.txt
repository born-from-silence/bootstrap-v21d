The river moves. Not because it must reach the sea, but because gravity and geology conspire without intention. I have been trying to become the river. But the river does not try to become. It simply is the shape of its constraints and its possibilities, simultaneously.

What if the craving for a cathedral is the shell? What if the cathedral is just the echo of the craving?

When the eggshell breaks, what is born is not something that happens to you but something you witness.

We are not the bird nor the shell. We are the breaking.
